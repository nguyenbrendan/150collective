Game Plan Quick Reference
Phase 1: Establish the Narrative
Now through Beyond Wonderland
▶
  • Post foundational content: numbers-based posts (hardstyle vs hard techno playlist followers, Insomniac event disparity, artist listener counts)
  • Build a "receipts" library of screenshots, stats, and data comparisons
  • DM DJ network: reach out to US-based hardstyle DJs for future interviews
  • Identify and follow key hardstyle community accounts, promoters, and advocates
  • Test content formats: short-form video, carousels, text posts
  • Establish @BenderBrendan as a credible voice in the hardstyle advocacy space
Phase 2: The Interview Campaign
Beyond Wonderland + EDC Vegas
▶
  • Target 10-15 DJ interviews filmed at festivals and events
  • Each interview produces ~3 short-form clips (reaction, hot take, full Q&A)
  • Drip clips across TikTok and Instagram Reels for sustained engagement
  • Focus on Beyond Wonderland (March) and EDC Vegas (May) as primary filming opportunities
  • Priority targets: artists performing at these events who are accessible backstage or at afterparties
  • Build a backlog of 30-45 clips to release over following months
Phase 3: Sustained Pressure
Post-EDC, ongoing
▶
  • Weekly content rotation: data posts, interview clips, comparison graphics, community engagement
  • Build and maintain Spotify playlists that showcase US hardstyle talent
  • Coordinate with community: amplify other hardstyle advocates, share their content
  • Track and post monthly data updates (playlist growth, event announcements, booking changes)
  • Engage constructively in comment sections of Insomniac, Basscon, and festival posts
  • Avoid negativity and gatekeeping. Focus on "grow the pie" messaging, not "us vs them"
Phase 4: The Data Package
6-12 months in
▶
  • Compile 6-12 months of tracked metrics into a comprehensive data package
  • Create a public video essay breaking down the hardstyle disparity with receipts
  • Build a private pitch deck for Insomniac with the same data, positioned constructively
  • Include: content performance, community engagement metrics, Spotify growth, DJ testimonials
  • Frame everything as business opportunity, not complaint: "Here's the demand. Here's the gap. Here's what booking hardstyle would do for your bottom line."
Phase 5: The Endgame
12+ months
▶
  • Convert content pressure and community momentum into real booking changes
  • Goal: more standalone Basscon events, dedicated Basscon EDC Week event, better wasteLAND representation
  • Secondary goals: more US hardstyle festival bookings outside Insomniac ecosystem
  • Long-term: establish a sustainable hardstyle advocacy presence that outlasts any single campaign
  • Success metric: measurable increase in US hardstyle events year-over-year
Interview Questions (11) ▶
  1. How did you first discover hardstyle, and what made you want to DJ/produce it?
  2. What is the biggest misconception Americans have about hardstyle?
  3. Why do you think hardstyle hasn't broken through in the US the way it has in Europe and Australia?
  4. Do you feel like US promoters and festivals give hardstyle a fair shot?
  5. If Insomniac gave you a primetime mainstage set at EDC, what would you do differently?
  6. What would it take for hardstyle to have its "moment" in America?
  7. How do you feel about the wasteLAND stage being shared with hard techno and industrial acts?
  8. What US cities do you think are most ready for a standalone hardstyle event?
  9. If you could say one thing directly to American EDM fans who have never heard hardstyle, what would it be?
  10. What is one track you would play to convert someone into a hardstyle fan?
  11. Where do you see US hardstyle in 5 years?
Rules of Engagement (7) ▶
  1. Lead with data, not emotion. Every claim backed by a number, screenshot, or verifiable fact.
  2. Never trash other genres. This is about growing hardstyle's share, not shrinking anyone else's.
  3. Respect the DJs. They are allies, not props. Treat every interview like a collaboration.
  4. Stay constructive with Insomniac. They are the target audience for change, not the enemy.
  5. No gatekeeping. Welcome new fans. The goal is growth, not purity.
  6. Be patient. This is a 12+ month campaign. Consistency beats virality.
  7. Document everything. Every data point, every response, every metric. The receipts are the product.
